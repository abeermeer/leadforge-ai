System Design — v2

Trax9 AI Lead Generation & Outreach

An automated pipeline that reads the agency's own website to learn what it sells, finds businesses that match that profile, audits each one's site, ads and SEO, then writes and sends outreach that references what it actually found.

Email Finder

Discovery returns a website, not an inbox. This stage finds someone to write to, stopping at the first confident hit. Leads with no address are flagged for manual entry rather than dropped.

1 · Scrape the siteContact and about pages, mailto links. Highest confidence.
2 · Pattern guessBuilt from a named person. Marked low confidence.
3 · Hunter.ioOptional, only if the operator supplies a key.

Lead Scoring

Every audited lead gets a fit score from 0 to 100 against the agency's ideal client, with the reasons kept alongside it so the operator can see why.

Scores higher

  • Weak site — they need the work
  • Already buying ads — budget exists
  • Industry matches the profile
  • Contactable

Scores lower

  • Site already excellent
  • Enterprise — unlikely to convert
  • No email found

AI Email Writer

Picks the single agency service that best solves the lead's biggest gap and leads with it — a shop running ads onto a slow page hears about the page, not about SEO. Every email cites something the audit actually found.

Reads in

  • Agency services
  • Full audit + top gap
  • Social signals

Writes out

  • Subject naming a real finding
  • Three to four paragraphs
  • Signed by a named consultant

Send Pipeline

Four gates before anything leaves. Any one of them can hold a message back.

Rate limitsHourly and daily, counted in Redis so every worker agrees.
WarmupStarts at 10 a day, climbs by 5 until it reaches the real cap.
SuppressionUnsubscribes, spam reports and hard bounces are never contacted again.
QuotaMonthly ceiling per account. Overrun pauses the campaign.
Then SendGrid sends it — with an unsubscribe link, a one-click unsubscribe header and a postal address in the footer, as the law requires.

After the send

Everything that comes back changes what happens next.
Inbound signals
Opens and clicks
Recorded against the lead from SendGrid's event webhook, verified by signature and de-duplicated by event id.
Bounces and spam reports
Added to the suppression list automatically. That address is finished.
Replies
Detected by polling the inbox, or by inbound parse. Matched back to the original message.
Follow-up sequence
Day 3 — second insight
Not a bump. A different finding from the same audit.
Day 7 — close the loop
Short, final, no pressure.
Cancels on any reply
A reply, unsubscribe or bounce cancels every remaining step for that lead immediately. Nobody gets chased after answering.

Applies to every stage

These wrap the pipeline rather than sit inside it.
Access & secrets
Accounts
Sign-in issues a token; every request is scoped to the account that made it. No query can reach another account's leads.
Encrypted keys
Third-party credentials are encrypted before storage and decrypted only in the moment they are used.
Staying unblocked
Rotation
Every browser run goes out through a rotating proxy and user-agent pool.
Backing off
Politeness delays per site; repeated blocks pause that source rather than hammer it.
Visibility
Progress
Long jobs report item by item, so a running campaign shows real progress and real failures.
Health
A health endpoint, structured logs and error reporting across the app and its workers.
